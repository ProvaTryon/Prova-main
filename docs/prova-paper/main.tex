\noindent{\fontsize{11}{13.2}\selectfont\bfseries Abstract}
\vspace{0.2cm}

\noindent{\fontsize{11}{13.2}\selectfont\justifying
Online fashion retail continues to suffer from elevated return rates driven primarily by the disconnect between how garments appear in static product imagery and how they fit individual consumers. This paper introduces \projectname{}, an end-to-end AI-driven fashion e-commerce platform that tackles this challenge through four tightly integrated capabilities: (i) a multi-pipeline virtual try-on system anchored by Prova-VTON, a diffusion-based model custom-trained on the MVHumanNet multi-view dataset for view-consistent garment synthesis; (ii) a monocular body measurement module built upon the SHAPY 3D mesh regressor, enhanced with a novel weight-based circumference correction factor; (iii) an interactive 360-degree garment preview engine powered by 3D Gaussian splatting; and (iv) a conversational fashion assistant underpinned by a five-stage Retrieval-Augmented Generation (RAG) pipeline that performs hybrid semantic--metadata search over the product catalog. The entire system is realised as a service-oriented architecture comprising Next.js, Express, and FastAPI microservices, and exposes a fully bilingual (English/Arabic) storefront. Quantitative evaluation against three state-of-the-art baselines on the MVHumanNet benchmark demonstrates that Prova-VTON attains 95.69\% and 96.92\% human preference in visual quality and geometric alignment, respectively, while maintaining competitive texture fidelity (DINOsim = 0.623). Latency profiling confirms that all interactive pipelines operate within their design-time targets, and usability studies validate the coherence of the integrated experience.
}
